IMPACT IDEATHON

hosted by united engineers & stemchats

Work together to identify and propose solutions to problems that will shape our future.

June 19-21, 2020

Change the World

By 2050, the world will need to feed 9 billion people. The number of people living in cities will triple. One third to one half of rain forests will vanish. Diseases will be deadlier than ever. The Impact Ideathon is hopefully the first of many Virtual Design Thinking Challenges hosted by United Engineers and STEMchats. The Impact Ideathon is a competition for students ages 16-18 who will collaborate to identify the world's greatest sustainable development challenges and propose solutions that will shape our future. Throughout this Ideathon, teams will select an idea to develop, justify, and present as a solution in response to a problem. Projects can take the form of computer programs, 3D models, graphic designs, pure academic research, or any other medium which can be tested and innovated upon. Participants will use design thinking and the engineering design process to evolve their solution to something that can be implemented.

Students do not need to have any prior experience in order to participate. They should just be willing to learn and take advantage of the workshops and speakers that will be offered. We will have workshops, keynote sessions, and guest speakers from the United Nations, National Geographic, Kale & Flax, and more. Through this Challenge, students will gain critical skills in teamwork, design thinking, and presentation as well as opportunities to network with leaders in the the social impact space as they relate to the STEM fields. We hope that participants will continue to advance their projects after the Challenge so that their solution may become accessible to the public for the good of all.

Welcome! Impact Ideathon is similar to hackathons and makeathons, except you only need the ability to think and the will to problem solve in response to a challenge. Like any competition, there are rules, forms, prompts, judging, and prizes. Everyone first registers individually. Because this Ideathon requires participants to work in teams of 2-4, you will eventually need to find other registered participants to work with you on the challenge. However, teams will not be final until June 19. Whether or not you have a team, you will be able to meet and work with people by joining the Slack workspace.
On June 19, everyone will attend an Opening Ceremony where the United Engineers will present more details on United Engineers, STEMchats, the Impact Ideathon challenge, team registration, and more. A guest speaker will address attendees to kick off the start of the challenge, offer good luck, and serve as inspiration. After the Opening Ceremony, participants will submit final team information. If participants do not have a team, they will be assigned on a team with similar interests.
Teams will have time from June 19 10:00 PM EST to June 21 12:00 PM EST to decide on the prompt they will address, do research, and develop a solution that addresses problems mentioned in the prompt using a variety of media. Teams may choose to write computer code, conduct academic research and compile an insightful paper, create 3D models and prototypes of a tangible solution, design graphics to raise awareness, and more! Because this is an Ideathon, the projects are not limited to a single common subject.
Despite this variety in projects, all teams will be judged on common criteria. In addition, all teams must submit project files in support of a final presentation by 12:00 PM EST on Sunday, June 21 for their entries to be eligible. All teams must prepare a 5-7 minute presentation they will share with a panel of judges on Sunday, June 21 during a designated time slot.
After all of the teams have been evaluated and a Closing Ceremony speaker has addressed participants, the judges will announce the top three teams which will receive first, second, and third place. A People‚Äôs Choice award will be given to a project and team elected for its outstanding qualities by other Ideathon participants.

Aside from the opportunity to win e-gift prizes, participants will have the opportunity to network with professionals in the social impact space, learn new design thinking and innovation skills, and collaborate with peers on creating a STEM project that addresses United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (UNSDGs).
